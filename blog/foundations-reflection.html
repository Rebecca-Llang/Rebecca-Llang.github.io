What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the Human Skills learning?

The role my ego plays in my self-esteem and how it can block me from fully absorbing and internalising content. I tried to speed through things, and then would have big blocks that I would struggle to overcome because of a lack of patience for the process and compassion for my struggles. This would then impact my confidence, as I'd set such high expectations for myself, rather than accepting the content and challenges.

That prioritising myself meant actively working through responsibilities and expectations at home, and making plans and options so that we can tackle new challenges both together and separately.

I struggled asking for help a lot more than I thought I would, with feelings of being a burden or annoying, for struggling with the same things over and over. Accepting and letting go of these feelings of shame and being unable, so I could move forward and learn (ie not yet/growth mindset) took some time. I can see how my ego can really get in the way of me engaging and participating, and the anxiety around me not understanding something is so intense. I’ve been working through it and really trying to ask for what I need before I feel like I'm in a crisis.

What are the roles of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Emotional safety and security for yourself and for your team.

Accessibility and teamwork through code readability and being able to share and build upon each other's work.

Accessibility and clarity in app design for users.

The ability to manage your own emotions so you can be in a space to problem solve and work through challenges without overwhelm or giving up. Healthy work and life culture, honouring Te Tiriti.

Create connections, share knowledge and build a better world.

What has surprised you the most about the Human Skills learning?

I felt really well versed when it came to how I treated and engaged with others, and it really highlighted how much I needed to apply that to my relationship with myself more consistently. I thought that over the last few years I had really learnt the skills that allowed me to look after myself, however I’ve seen that I used them by making my life smaller, and now I’m learning how to adapt them to fit in with new challenges. I really liked the habits and building behaviour models, and can see why I can slip as I often try to do everything at once. Celebrating the wins, like having a consistent bed-time and rising routine and bringing back yoga and alone time. Reminding myself that I’m in a time of big change, both with Devs and moving into a new place with my partner, and that I know what my biggest struggles are, and I know ways in which I can work to improve them.

What were the most challenging aspects of the Human Skills learning?

For me, it was being honest and vulnerable while trying to decide the limitations that I was comfortable with. I did find it confronting, as stigma and discrimination does exist, and I didn’t quite know how to navigate it honestly, while also protecting my privacy and boundaries. Luckily, I cried about it in an open co-working space with a facilitator (not clicking it was public), which another student accidentally was in, so it was the fear itself being realised. This made me laugh, and took a lot of pressure off, as I’d already done the thing I was worried about and everything was fine.

Why Humans Skills in programming?

It’s important in all aspects of life. People, and individuals, all matter and the world is a better place with diversity, compassion and connection. The mental health of the individual, and the collective, strengthens and lifts us all, and the impact will make the community a safer and happier place.

I had some moments thinking I would speed through Human Skills as I consider it to be one of my strengths. However through the journey, and the very obvious lack of compassion, patience and acceptance of my struggles shows how much I still have to go. I did find it quite upsetting at the start, as I felt it was hard to establish the boundaries of what I wanted and didn’t want to share, however it really pushed me to make some necessary adjustments and have some very frank and honest conversations in my new living space and dynamic. I can see where I have work to do, and I think it’ll be so incredibly helpful for me to adapt into spaces that don’t have such a strong focus on human skills, as I will be stronger in self-regulating, and letting things go.
